Welcome to ARCHI-AID Learning Hub — A space where ideas, knowledge, and creativity come together. Here, we break down core architectural concepts and real-world designs into accessible lessons.

Design Case Studies

Case Study: Modern Residential Architecture

Analyzing functional layouts, form studies, and residential space planning using clean geometry.

Core Principles

01. Primary Form & Massing

Understanding subtractive form, solid geometry, and utilizing cutouts in 3D volumes to achieve built-in environmental shading.

02. Interlocking Spatial Relationships

How overlapping concrete terraces wrap around landscaping to integrate internal functions smoothly with nature.

03. Horizontal Planar Elements

Utilizing sweeping roof planes to define boundaries and columns to emphasize open, light visual horizons.

04. Material Contrast & Texture

Juxtaposing rugged stone facades against warm timber paneling to create functional zones without physical barriers.

05. Rhythm, Pattern, and Repetition

Using repeating sequences of structural slats along walls and ceilings to establish dynamic spatial movement.

06. Axis & Linear Spatial Paths

Organizing linear column rows along an invisible centerline to frame paths and intuitively guide human circulation.

07. Monolithic Brutalism & Geometry

Turning functional features like staircases into sculptures by celebrating the raw, uncovered strength of concrete.

08. The Integration of Green Architecture

Applying biophilic architecture principles by integrating deep plant beds into structures for climate balance and natural cooling.

09. Spatial Enclosure & Transparency

Balancing solid protective floor-roof frameworks with vast transparent glass elements to open up interior volumes.

10. Cantilever & Structural Defiance

Engineering extended volumes hanging over open spaces without support pillars to form sheltered outdoor patios underneath.

11. Spatial Enclosure & Volumetric Intersect

Creating geometric, multi-level framing cutouts beneath overhead skylights to channel illumination deep into lower levels.

12. Linear Rhythms & Structural Enclosures

Arranging sequences of columns and overhead framing rafters to visually enclose transition walkways without using solid walls.

13. Planar Intersections & Asymmetric Massing

Weaving flat, overlapping planes together dynamically to move away from rigid boundaries into sculptural layouts.
